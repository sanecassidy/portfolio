Heavy Juggling Balls

Inception

Stanford University's Design and Manufacturing course (ME 203) includes an open-ended project in which each student is encouraged to define, design, prototype, and manufacture an object of their own conception. The object can be just about anything so long as, by bringing it into existence, the student demonstrates a facility with the manufacturing tools available to them at Stanford's Product Realization Laboratory (or PRL).

Concept

A set of weighted juggling balls, each with a near-solid steel core, coated by a layer of translucent silicone. Holes in the steel core that were added to enable fixturing while it was being turned down to a sphere are hidden by a unique, custom-designed glyph shape, also molded in silicone. In addition to hiding the fixturing holes, the glyph, when seen from different angles and reflections, can be seen to spell my name.

Tools

  • SolidWorks
  • Manual mill (including rotating table)
  • Manual lathe (including radius cutter)
  • FDM 3D printer
  • Vacuum chamber

Materials

  • Steel cylinder, 3.5" thick
  • Modulan block, 8"x8"x4"
  • 2-part RTV silicone
  • ABS filament for FDM printing
  • Clear nail polish
